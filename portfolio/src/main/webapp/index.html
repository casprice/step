About Me

I am a software developer from San Gabriel, CA. I'm currently working from home as a Google STEP intern, but I am also an undergraduate student attending UC San Diego.

I first started learning about the underlying components of a web page in middle school. However, it wasn’t until my senior year of high school that I formalized my learning of Computer Science through my AP CS class and by following online web development tutorials.

Nowadays, I love exploring the many different facets of Computer Science by attending hackathons, writing software for my robotics lab, and learning the math and engineering behind artificial intelligence. I also enjoy bringing this knowledge to my peers through my student org, ACM UCSD, because I know how overwhelming it can be to get started in such a vast field. In fact, one of my goals for the 2020-21 school year is to start up an outreach program within ACM UCSD, to help familiarize young students and minorities in tech run with CS fundamentals.

In my free time, my favorite things to do include running and hiking with friends, reading, and solving crossword puzzles with my sister.
